title: ECOA and Regulation B — prohibited basis and disparate impact
source: "Equal Credit Opportunity Act (15 USC 1691); Regulation B (12 CFR 1002)"
provenance: public
citation: "ECOA / Regulation B, 12 CFR 1002"
---

The Equal Credit Opportunity Act prohibits discrimination in any aspect of a
credit transaction on a prohibited basis, including race, color, religion,
national origin, sex, marital status, age (provided the applicant has capacity to
contract), and the receipt of public assistance.

Regulation B implements ECOA. A creditor generally may not request or use a
prohibited-basis characteristic as a factor in a credit decision, with narrow
exceptions (for example, age may be used in an empirically derived, demonstrably
and statistically sound credit scoring system in limited ways). Using a variable
that is a close proxy for a prohibited basis can raise the same concern as using
the basis directly.

Two theories of liability are relevant to models: disparate treatment (using a
prohibited basis) and disparate impact (a facially neutral policy that has a
disproportionate adverse effect on a protected group and is not justified by
business necessity, or where a less discriminatory alternative exists). A model
review should exclude prohibited bases and their proxies from inputs, and test
outcomes across protected groups.
