---
id: four-fifths-rule
title: The four-fifths (80%) rule for adverse impact
source: "EEOC Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection Procedures, 29 CFR 1607.4(D)"
provenance: public
citation: "Four-Fifths Rule, 29 CFR 1607.4(D)"
---

A selection rate for any group that is less than four-fifths (80 percent) of the
rate for the group with the highest rate is generally regarded as evidence of
adverse impact. Selection rates that are within four-fifths of each other are
generally not regarded as adverse impact.

Applied to a credit model, the rule compares the favorable-outcome rate across
protected groups. A disparity ratio below 0.80 is a signal that warrants review
of whether the disparity is justified by a legitimate, non-discriminatory factor,
or whether a less discriminatory alternative exists.

The rule is a screening threshold, not a safe harbor: a ratio at or above 0.80
does not by itself prove the absence of discrimination, and a ratio below 0.80
does not by itself prove discrimination. It flags cases for further analysis.
